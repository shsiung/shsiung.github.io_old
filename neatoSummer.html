Summer 2013 Computer Science Robotics Research

During the summer of 2013, two undergraduate students and I participated in a research focusing on writing algorithms for the off-the-shelf robot - Neato (official website here ).

We used the Robot Operating System (ROS) as our core communicator between the computer and the robot. We wrote a driver in Python that retrieves data from the Neato, and used the 360 degree laser scanner data to construct a local environment scanning:

In above picture, the center is where the robot is located. The up-direction is were the robot is facing (in this case, it is facing a wall). And we can see clearly, the robot is currently at a T-shaped intersection. We then uses the laser data to do corridor navigation:

We then hand drew a map of the environment, and uses techniques such as breadth-first-search to construct a path from start to goal. Therefore we made a map-based navigation:

Now we got a map and navigation, we then did a few different kinds of localization. This allow us to put the robot anywhere on the map, and the robot will drive around to figure out where it is currently.
